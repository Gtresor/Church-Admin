# 01 — Project Overview

## Proposed Thesis Topic
Design and Implementation of a Church Sacramental and Member Management Information System Using Django.

## Background Problem
Many churches manage sacramental records (baptism, baby dedication, wedding), member profiles, and certificates using paper files or disconnected spreadsheets. This leads to delayed approvals, inconsistent records, difficult reporting, and weak traceability of certificates.

## General Objective
To develop a web-based system that centralizes sacramental workflows, member management, and certificate issuance/verification for a church environment.

## Specific Objectives
1. Digitize member and child profile records with controlled access.
2. Automate baptism, dedication, and wedding request/review workflows.
3. Generate certificates in PDF format with unique serial numbers and QR-based verification.
4. Provide administrative dashboards, charts, and exports for decision support.
5. Support member self-service requests and profile updates.

## Project Scope
### In Scope
- Role-based portals (admin/staff and member)
- Member and child profile management
- Baptism, baby dedication, and wedding lifecycle handling
- Certificate generation, listing, revocation, and public verification
- Reports, calendar view, CSV/PDF exports
- Internal prompt-driven reporting assistant (rule-based)

### Out of Scope
- Online payments
- SMS/email notification gateway integration
- Multi-church tenancy
- Advanced machine learning prediction

## Significance of the Study
- Improves operational efficiency and data consistency.
- Reduces manual errors in sacramental records.
- Improves accountability through certificate traceability and revocation handling.
- Supports data-driven pastoral/administrative decisions through reports.

## Problem Statement (Draft Paragraph)
This project addresses inefficiencies in manual church record management by introducing a unified web system that manages members, sacramental workflows, and certificate control. It ensures structured data capture, status-based approvals, and exportable analytics, while providing public certificate authenticity checks.
